Decoding strategies for the conscientious shopper

Read in this order

  • If the ingredient list is longer than your hand, put it back.
  • Anything starting with E1__ to E4__ is usually a permitted additive — look it up rather than assume the worst, but treat as a signal of processing.
  • "Natural flavour" can legally hide dozens of components; in highly processed foods, assume the flavour profile is engineered.
  • "Uncured" and "no nitrites added" with celery powder/juice deliver the same nitrite exposure as cured meats.

Common hidden synonyms

  • Sugar: HFCS, glucose-fructose syrup, isoglucose, maltose, dextrose, agave, fruit juice concentrate, evaporated cane juice.
  • MSG: hydrolysed vegetable protein, yeast extract, autolysed yeast, sodium caseinate, soy protein isolate.
  • Carrageenan: E407, Irish moss extract, PNG carrageenan, "vegetable gum."
  • Nitrites: celery powder, celery juice concentrate, "natural curing agents."
  • Synthetic dyes: E102 (Yellow 5), E110 (Yellow 6), E129 (Red 40), E133 (Blue 1), E143 (Green 3).
